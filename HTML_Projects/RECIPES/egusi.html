EGUSI SOUP

Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian dish, and its origin can be traced back to Yorubaland. [1][2][3] It is made round melon seeds. [4] It is commonly enjoyed in countries such as Nigeria, Ghana, and Cameroon. The soup is known for its rich flavor and thick, hearty consistency

  1. Prepare Egusi: Place egusi seeds (or pumpkin seeds) in a blender; blend until the mixture is powdery, 30 to 40 seconds. Set aside.
  2. Prepare and Cook Beef: Cut beef into bite-sized cubes and season with salt. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Cook beef in hot oil until brown but not cooked through, 3 to 5 minutes.
  3. Prepare and Cook Tomato Mixture: Place tomatoes, onion, and peppers in a blender; blend until smooth, about 30 seconds. Stir the tomato mixture into the beef; reduce heat to medium-low and cover. Cook until the meat is tender, 40 to 50 minutes.
  4. Add Liquids and Seafood: Add shrimp, tomato sauce, water, and tomato paste; simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Add Final Ingredients: Stir in the spinach and the powdered egusi seeds. Continue simmering for 10 more minutes.
